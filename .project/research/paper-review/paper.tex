\begin{abstract}
Mapping fermionic creation and annihilation operators to qubit Pauli
strings is a prerequisite for quantum simulation of molecular and
condensed-matter systems.  Five encodings dominate the literature:
Jordan--Wigner, Bravyi--Kitaev, Parity, balanced binary tree, and
balanced ternary tree.  The Seeley--Richard--Love (SRL) index-set
framework is widely cited as a unified treatment, expressing the first
three as instances of a single parameterized map.

This paper provides a self-contained tutorial review of the
fermion-to-qubit encoding landscape, tracing the development from the
1928 Jordan--Wigner transformation through modern tree-based methods.
We present two encoding paradigms --- index-set schemes and path-based
tree encodings --- in a unified notation and compare their domains of
validity.

Our main original result is the \emph{star-tree theorem}: the generic
index-set construction, applied to a labelled rooted tree, satisfies
the canonical anticommutation relations if and only if the tree is a
star (depth~$\le 1$).  This reveals that the SRL unification is far
narrower than assumed, covering only relabelled Jordan--Wigner and
Parity encodings.  The Bravyi--Kitaev encoding uses a separate,
Fenwick-specific construction that is algebraically distinct from the
generic method.

We identify three construction-compatibility regions in the space of
$n^{n-1}$ labelled rooted trees, establish the path-based construction
as the only universal method, and validate all results by exhaustive
symbolic computation.
\end{abstract}
