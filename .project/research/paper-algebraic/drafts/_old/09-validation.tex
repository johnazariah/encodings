\subsection{Eigenspectrum equivalence}
\label{sec:val-eigen}

We encode the H$_2$ STO-3G Hamiltonian (4~spin-orbitals, bond
length $R = 0.7414\;\text{\AA}$) with all five encodings:
Jordan--Wigner (JW), Bravyi--Kitaev (BK), Parity, balanced binary
tree (BinTree), and balanced ternary tree (TerTree).  Each produces a
15-term qubit Hamiltonian with identical summed absolute coefficients
$\sum_k |c_k| = 3.2557$.

Full $16 \times 16$ matrix diagonalisation confirms that all five
encodings yield identical eigenspectra to machine precision:
$|\Delta\lambda| = 4.44 \times 10^{-16}$.  The ground-state energy is
$E_0 = -1.7622\;\text{Ha}$ (electronic FCI), giving
$E_\text{total} = -1.0471\;\text{Ha}$ with nuclear repulsion.

The JW encoding produces the expected excitation terms
$\pm 0.1744\; XXYY$, $\pm 0.1744\; YYXX$, while the ternary tree
distributes these across different Pauli signatures ($XXXY$, $XXYX$,
$XYXX$, $XYYY$) — the same physics in a different representation.

\subsection{Anticommutation verification}
\label{sec:val-car}

For each encoding, we construct the full set of $2n$ Majorana
operators and verify all $\binom{2n}{2}$ anticommutators both
symbolically (via the Pauli algebra) and by matrix computation
at small~$n$.  The protocol is detailed in \cref{app:car-verification}.

\begin{itemize}
  \item $n = 4, 8, 16$: all five path-based encodings (JW, BK,
    Parity, BinTree, TerTree) satisfy the CAR with zero deviation.
  \item $n = 8$, balanced ternary tree with Construction~A:
    $\acomm{\an{4}}{\ad{7}} \neq 0$, confirming the star-tree
    restriction (\cref{thm:star-tree}).
  \item $n = 8$, Fenwick tree with Construction~A (generic
    index-set computation, \emph{not} the BK-specific
    Construction~F formulas): CAR violated — confirming that
    Construction~F is distinct from Construction~A applied to a
    Fenwick-shaped tree.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Parity operator weights}
\label{sec:val-parity}

Using symbolic Pauli algebra (no matrices), we compute the encoded
parity operator $\hat{P} = \encode\bigl(\prod_{j=0}^{n-1}
(I - 2\nhat{j})\bigr)$ for each encoding.  All parity operators are
single Pauli strings ($\hat{P}^2 = I$ verified symbolically).

\begin{table}[t]
\centering
\caption{Parity operator $\hat{P}$ weight and structure.}
\label{tab:parity}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Encoding} & $\pauliw{\hat{P}}$ & \textbf{Structure} \\
\midrule
Jordan--Wigner     & $n$            & $Z^{\otimes n}$ \\
Parity             & $1$            & $Z_{n-1}$ (single qubit) \\
Balanced binary    & $1$            & $Z_\text{root}$ \\
Bravyi--Kitaev     & $O(\log n)$    & $Z$ on Fenwick path \\
Balanced ternary   & $O(\log_3 n)$  & $Z$ on root path \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

This computation scales to $n = 100$ in under 6~seconds using purely
symbolic algebra, demonstrating the library's practical utility for
systems beyond toy models.

\subsection{Star-tree census}
\label{sec:val-census}

We enumerate all $n^{n-1}$ labelled rooted trees for $n = 1, \ldots,
6$ via Pr\"ufer sequences, classify each as monotonic or
non-monotonic, and test Construction~A against the full CAR.

\begin{table}[t]
\centering
\caption{Monotonicity census and Construction~A domain.  $|M(n)|$ =
  monotonic trees; ``Pass CAR'' = trees where Construction~A
  satisfies all anticommutation relations.}
\label{tab:monotonicity}
\begin{tabular}{@{}rrrrr@{}}
\toprule
$n$ & $n^{n-1}$ & $|M(n)|$ & $(n{-}1)!$ & Pass CAR \\
\midrule
1 &       1 &     1 &     1 &  1 \\
2 &       2 &     1 &     1 &  2 \\
3 &       9 &     2 &     2 &  3 \\
4 &      64 &     6 &     6 &  4 \\
5 &     625 &    24 &    24 &  5 \\
6 &    7776 &   120 &   120 &  6 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Two results are visible in \cref{tab:monotonicity}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $|M(n)| = (n{-}1)!$ exactly, for every $n$ tested
    (\cref{prop:monotonic-count}).
  \item The number of trees passing the CAR under Construction~A
    is exactly~$n$ — the $n$ possible star trees.  This is strictly
    less than $(n{-}1)!$ for $n \ge 4$, confirming that monotonicity
    is necessary but not sufficient (\cref{thm:star-tree}).
\end{enumerate}

This exhaustive verification provides strong computational evidence
for the star-tree theorem and establishes the precise boundary between
the domains of Construction~A and Construction~B.
